# Executive Summary — Zomato Profitability Diagnostic

## Situation
Zomato has grown revenue from INR 1,994 Cr (FY21) to INR 7,079 Cr (FY23) — a 255% increase over 3 years. Yet EBITDA margins remain deeply negative at -9.5% in FY23, suggesting the business has a structural cost problem, not a revenue problem.

## Complication
Costs are growing faster than revenue. Delivery costs alone consumed **57.8% of revenue in FY23**, up from 45% in FY21. Customer acquisition costs have increased **2.1x** relative to FY21 levels, while retention metrics have not improved proportionally.

## Key Question
**Where exactly is the margin leaking, and what levers can recover it?**

## Findings

### Finding 1 — Delivery cost is the primary margin killer
- Delivery costs grew **34% YoY** vs revenue growth of **22%** in FY23
- Every incremental order is more expensive to deliver than the previous year
- Root cause: low average order value (INR 410) relative to fixed delivery cost per order

### Finding 2 — CAC has doubled without proportional retention gains
- CAC index: 100 (FY21) → 178 (FY22) → 210 (FY23)
- High churn means spend on acquiring customers is not being recovered
- Blanket discounting is driving orders, not loyalty

### Finding 3 — Scale is not improving unit economics
- GOV grew from INR 11,048 Cr to INR 26,574 Cr (+140%)
- Yet EBITDA margin barely improved: -9.1% → -10.5% → -9.5%
- Fixed cost leverage is not materialising — variable costs scaling faster

## Recommendations

| Lever | Action | Projected Impact |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| B2B Catering | Expand corporate/office delivery (high AOV, batched) | -8–12% delivery cost ratio |
| Dynamic Discounting | Replace blanket offers with ML-targeted promotions | -15–20% CAC |
| Dark Kitchen Partnerships | Reduce last-mile distance via micro-fulfillment | +4–6pp EBITDA |

**Combined projected EBITDA recovery: ~12%**

## Conclusion
Zomato's margin problem is solvable — but requires a fundamental shift from volume-driven growth to unit-economics-driven growth. The three levers above address the root causes directly and can be implemented within 2–3 quarters.
